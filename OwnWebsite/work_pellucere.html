Pellucere website

The Brief

A website design aimed at women aged over 30 who have self-awareness, who are confident, happy to spend a bit more on a quality product, concerned about the purity of what they use on their skin, experienced with existing skin care and who want good skincare results. They know their own style and want something elegant to complement and bring affordable luxury and elegance to their lives. The products are organic, have an emphasis on purity, quality, are petrochemical free, organic, fairtrade, hand-made, environmentally friendly, and contain no use of unnatural oils to prevent harming the skin.

Outcome

"After first assessing my product and brand needs, Indu found creative, original solutions to web design problems. Her knowledge of internet product placement and her enthusiasm made the process enjoyable and stress free. I could rely on Indu to deliver quality and be responsive to any changes" - Sophie, Owner of Pellucere

Building the new e-commerce website

New Information Architecture (IA)

A new website design following relevant ISO guidelines, UX best practice, including Nielsen's usability heuristics, from interviewing women from the 30+ age range as well as other women, and from talking and listening to women buying products at various stores that sell organic beauty products for women.

A website design that exceeded the client brief via:

- a design portraying elegance and self-assured women via the site images, colour and layout

- a minimalist design portraying the link between the product and nature, environmental friendliness (the products are hand made, petrochemical free, organic, and environmentally friendly) connecting via the site images, colour and layout

- designing the Information Architecture (IA) from user research into the areas: 'Our vision', 'Our products', 'What's new', 'Skincare tips', 'Ingredients', 'Contact us'

- navigation tab 'Our vision' to emphasise the ethos behind the company, as well as the purity of the products

- navigation tab 'Ingredients' to emphasise the natural-origin of the ingredients

- navigation tab 'Skincare tips' as a way to build and connect with the audience, giving skincare information



The Challenge

  • To accommodate quickly in the website design, the constantly changing form of this new start-up
  • To integrate in the website design, e-commerce features without any distraction from the client brief
  • A responsive design, to take account of mobile and tablet usage

Outline of my work

Designing a new Information Architecture (IA) to:
  • Highlight the company vision/ethos
  • Highlight the products for sale in an attractive, yet web usable manner

Website design included:

  • Research involved talking to women aged over 30 as potential users of the website rather than using personas/scenarios to ascertain the design goals, making sure the navigation was usable, easy to understand, usable, accessible and organised in an understandable way.
  • Observation and task analysis of paper prototypes to see how people can use the website design, real behaviour.
  • Website page 'mockups' for the functional specifications
  • Reviewed websites from Chanel, Helena Rubinstein, Active Concepts and Vogue that include a product focus on the target group women aged over 30, as well as The Body Shop, Origins UK and Arran Aromatics, and one example of a resulting outcome was the use of images rather than text on the website main page to entice, since the Pellucere brand name is not established
